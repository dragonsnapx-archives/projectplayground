Human Rights

Background

Out of the stereotypes that exist in current society, those related to single teenage mothers are some of the strongest, propelling many pregnant teenagers to have an abortion. Even in Korea, where abortion is mostly illegal, around 80% of pregnant teenagers have an abortion. On top of this social stigma, single teenage mothers face overwhelming financial problems. However, government support for teenage mothers falls well under the international human rights guidelines, forcing single teenage mothers to give up their children to orphanages.

Our Response

Playground’s Humans Rights Committee organized a fundraiser and raised over $3000, supplying necessities such as shampoo and powdered milk to the Korean Unwed Mothers Families’ Association (KUMFA), an organization that provides shelter for 20 single mothers each year. Through this effort, Playground was able to speak up against the unjust institutional bias against single teenage mothers and encourage organizations such as KUMFA to push forward in the fight for social justice. Going beyond the scope of teenage single mothers, Playground also created a video about the various stereotypes that exist in our society and how to combat them.
